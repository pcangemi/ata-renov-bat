# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Homeowners and small businesses around Juan-les-Pins, Antibes and the Alpes-Maritimes who need electrical work: renovation, mise aux normes, dépannage, modernisation. They are non-technical, often browsing on a phone, and a meaningful share are 55+. Their job to be done is finding an electrician they can trust with their home — and reaching him without friction. The surface speaks directly to these prospective clients; the artisan (Atallah Hassani) is the subject, not the audience.

## Product Purpose

The relaunch site for ATA Rénov'Bat as Atallah restarts the activity in Juan-les-Pins. The site's single job is to convert local visitors into phone contact. Success is measured in calls (and WhatsApp messages) from new clients in the area.

## Positioning

One exacting independent electrician, accountable end-to-end — de la conception à la maintenance — who answers his own phone.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: call 07 58 50 51 42 (`tel:` links in nav, contact section, footer). Secondary: WhatsApp message (floating button + contact card) for visitors not ready to talk.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "un artisan électricien précis et fiable, à Juan-les-Pins, que je peux appeler directement."
- Belief ladder: (1) this is a real, active artisan near me → (2) he covers the work I need (prestations) → (3) his work is exacting (réalisations, the spec-sheet presentation itself) → (4) others vouch for him (4,9/5, six named Google reviews) → (5) reaching him is easy → call.
- Proof on hand: six verbatim Google reviews (4,9/5) in the Avis section; manufacturer partner logos (Schneider Electric, Legrand, Hager, Nexans, Otio); MAAF assurance décennale statement; real worksite photography.

## Brand Personality

Précis · fiable · artisanal. Calm technical confidence — the tone of a craftsman who documents his work, not a marketer who sells it. French voice, vouvoiement, short declarative sentences. The interface should evoke trust and competence, never urgency.

## Anti-references

- The old site's own look: a generic MUI/theFront SaaS template with stock everything — exactly what this rebuild replaces.
- Generic AI landing-page aesthetics ("no basic AI thing" — the owner's words): interchangeable hero-gradient-cards pages with no point of view.
- Pushy urgency marketing: countdowns, discount banners, popup chat prompts. One artisan's reputation doesn't shout.
- Wellness-soft cream aesthetics (serif italic, spa warmth): the wrong register for a trade; this is a technical craft, not hospitality.

## Design Principles

1. **The spec sheet is the proof.** Technical presentation — figure captions, schematic labels, precise alignment — is itself the argument that this artisan is exacting.
2. **Show the chantier.** Real worksite imagery over abstraction; the work is the brand.
3. **One call away.** Every fold offers a direct path to phone or WhatsApp; nothing sits more than one screen from a contact action.
4. **Calm confidence.** No urgency tricks; the reviews and the craft do the selling.
5. **Fast and light.** One page, no build step, instant load on a phone in the field.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Solid defaults, no formal WCAG target: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, full keyboard navigability, `prefers-reduced-motion` honored across all animation (GSAP, marquee, typed effect), touch targets comfortable on mobile. Bear the older clientele in mind: readable sizes, unambiguous labels, no interaction that depends on hover.
